When you cough or blow out candles, this is the muscle you are engaging.\n\n\n\nExternal Obliques\n\n\n\nThese are the large muscles that wrap around your rib cage starting just beneath your arms. They help you flex, bend, twist and rotate.\n\n\n\nInternal Obliques\n\n\n\nThese are deeper side muscles than the external obliques and help to stabilize you.\n\n\n\nRectus Abdominus\n\n\n\nThis is the one most people think of when they look at their abs because it is noticeable. It is the muscle that runs up the front of the body and looks like a 6-pack when super lean. The rectus abdominus helps to flex the spine as well as provide stabilization during side bending and while your extremities are moving.\n\n\n\n\nRelated: 9 Tips + Workout To Get Rid of Lower Belly Fat\n\n\n\nOur 15 Best Ab Exercises For Women\n\n\n\nKnowing all of that, we have chosen 15 of our best ab moves to include in this ab workout that tackle all four muscles of the abdominals. \n\n\n\nBelow, each of the ab exercises is explained and shown just for you! In just 8 short minutes you’ll be so glad you did!\n\n\n\nThe Best Ab Workout for Women\n\n\n\nV-ups\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nV-ups are one of the best ab workouts for women because they also develop incredible balance.\n\n\n\nHow to perform v-ups:\n\n\n\n1) Lie flat on the floor and extend your arms behind your head. Keep your feet together and toes pointed.\n\n\n\n2) Keep your legs straight and lift them up as you simultaneously raise your upper body off the floor. Keep your core tight as you reach for your toes with your hands. Slowly lower yourself back down to the starting position.\n\n\n\nTargets: transverse abdominus, rectus abdominus\n\n\n\nSide Plank Dips\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nSide plank dips are obliques exercises for women looking to develop core strength and enviable side abs.\n\n\n\nHow to perform side plank lift and lower:\n\n\n\n1) Start by laying on your right side with forearm below shoulder, body lifted and legs long and feet stacked. Keep body straight, abs tight and place left hand on hip.\n\n\n\n2) Dip your hips down towards the mat and lift back up using your obliques and core muscles.\n\n\n\nTargets: internal obliques, external obliques\n\n\n\nSide Plank Reach Through\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nThe side plank reach through is one of the best ab oblique combination movements. You must keep your core engaged the entire time – you’ll be feeling this move!\n\n\n\nHow to perform a side plank scoop:\n\n\n\n1) Begin in a full side plank position with right hand below shoulder, left arm reaching high above left shoulder and legs long. (Modify by placing bottom knee onto mat if needed)\n\n\n\n2) Tighten abdominals and slowly scoop left arm under the body, following with your eyes until you see behind you.\n\n\n\n3) Slowly reach left hand back to start position.\n\n\n\n4) Repeat for desired number of repetitions and switch sides.\n\n\n\nTargets: transverse abdominus, internal obliques, external obliques, rectus abdominus\n\n\n\nToe Reach\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nThe toe reach is different than the classic crunch you see in most ab workouts for women. This focuses on core engagement and the rectus abdominus and the transverse abdomunus.\n\n\n\nHow to perform toe touches with reach:\n\n\n\n1) Start on back with legs long reaching towards the ceiling.\n\n\n\n2) Bring head, neck, and shoulders off the mat and reach arms up towards the feet. Return to the tips of the shoulders and repeat.\n\n\n\nTargets: rectus abdominus, transverse abdominus\n\n\n\nPlank\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nThe plank is a staple part of most ab workouts because it recruits the entire core.\n\n\n\nHow to perform a plank:\n\n\n\n1) Begin kneeling on all fours with hand directly under shoulders and toes on the mat.\n\n\n\n2) Tighten abdominals and raise your body up off the floor, lengthening legs behind you. Hold entire body tight for desired amount of time and breathe.\n\n\n\nTargets: transverse abdominus\n\n\n\nBicycle Crunch\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nThe bicycle crunch builds coordination, a strong core, and is one of the best ab movements.\n\n\n\nHow to perform a bicycle:\n\n\n\n1) Start lying on back with feet off the floor and legs in a tabletop position. Hands are behind the head and head, neck, and shoulders are flexed off the mat.\n\n\n\n2) Bring one knee in and the opposite elbow over reaching towards it. Repeat on the other side in a criss cross motion working the obliques.\n\n\n\nTargets: transverse abdominus, internal obliques, external obliques, rectus abdominus\n\n\n\nBoat Pose\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nBoat pose is deceptively simple. This move takes incredible core strength and builds core strength. A win-win.\n\n\n\nHow to perform boat pose:\n\n\n\n1) From a seated position, draw naval to spine bracing abdominals and, with hands on floor behind you, bring your legs up off the floor with knees bent in front of the chest.\n\n\n\n2) Keeping abdominals tight, slowly lift hands off floor and reach them forward and up while simultaneously lengthening legs upward. Your body will look like a “V” shape. Lengthen spine as you hold. (If your hamstrings are too tight, bend knees slightly.)\n\n\n\n3) Keep abdominals tight and breathe. Stay for 30-60 seconds or as long as you can.\n\n\n\nTargets: transverse abdominus, rectus abdominus\n\n\n\nSweeping Scissors\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nHow to perform sweeping scissors:\n\n\n\n1) Start lying on the mat with arms over your head and legs long.\n\n\n\n2) Sweep arms out to sides and bring head, neck, and shoulders off the mat reaching arms long and lift one leg up and in towards the chest.  Reaching arms forward on the sides of the leg. Roll back to mat lowering the leg, and repeat on the other side.\n\n\n\nTargets: transverse abdominus, rectus abdominus\n\n\n\nSide To Side Obliques\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nThe side to side obliques move is one of the best ab exercises for women who want to target the obliques.\n\n\n\nHow to perform side to side obliques:\n\n\n\n1) Lie on mat and bring knees into the chest forming a tabletop position. Bring the head, neck and shoulder off the mat coming into crunch reaching arms long at the sides.\n\n\n\n2) Exhale as you reach right fingers to the right side and forward then return to center on the inhale. Exhale and reach left fingers to the left side and forward. Switch side to side without lowering onto the mat.\n\n\n\nTargets: transverse abdominus, internal obliques, external obliques, rectus abdominus\n\n\n\nBend Extend Ab Tuck\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nAb workouts don’t have to be boring. There’s always a new variation to try, just like this bend extend ab tuck.\n\n\n\nHow to perform bend extend ab tuck:\n\n\n\n1) Start seated on mat with arms behind you, hands on the mat and fingers facing your backside. Sit back on your hands and lift up your feet off the mat keeping the knees bent.\n\n\n\n2) Keep abs tight and press your legs out long and lower back down slightly. You should feel your lower abdominals working, then push yourself back up and pull your knees in to return to your starting position.\n\n\n\nTargets: transverse abdominus, rectus abdominus\n\n\n\nSpiderman Abs\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nHow do you flatten lower abs? This spiderman abs move is a start! It’s a move that hurts so good – give it a try and your lower abs will be at attention.\n\n\n\nHow to perform spiderman abs:\n\n\n\n1) Start in a plank position with hands on mat slightly wider than shoulders, legs long behind you and abdominals and glutes squeezed tight.\n\n\n\n2)  Pull the left knee toward the outside of your left elbow and hold for one count. Return to the starting and repeat on right side.\n\n\n\nTargets: transverse abdominus, external obliques, internal obliques\n\n\n\nForearm Star Plank\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nWondering how women can really strengthen their core? Through consistent core work and switching things up. This forearm star plank is a variation on the plank that will help you strengthen your core and even those lower abs that can be so hard to get.\n\n\n\nHow to perform forearm star plank:\n\n\n\n1) Begin lying on right side, forearm on the floor, body lifted, and legs extended long on the mat with feet stacked.\n\n\n\n2) Tighten abdominals and lift left leg and arm up in the air. Hold them straight and keep body still for desired amount of time. Switch sides.\n\n\n\nTargets: external obliques, internal obliques, transverse abdominus\n\n\n\nHip Dips\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nWhile no one workout can get rid of love handles (only fat loss and clean eating can do that – sorry!) hip dips really build those oblique muscles. Strong, defined obliques are muscles to be proud of.\n\n\n\nHow to perform hip dips:\n\n\n\n1) Begin in a forearm plank position with your forearms flat on the mat, making sure that your elbows are aligned directly under your shoulders, your core is engaged, and your body is in a straight line from your head to your feet.\n\n\n\n2) Lower your left hip toward the floor until it almost touches and then immediately raise back to center.\n\n\n\n3) Repeat on the right side.\n\n\n\nTarget: transverse abdominus, external obliques, internal obliques\n\n\n\nDouble Leg Stretch\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nHow to perform double leg stretch:\n\n\n\n1) Start lying on back with knees bent 90 degrees and arms lengthened alongside calves.  The low back should be pressed against the mat with head, neck and shoulders flexed up off the mat.\n\n\n\n2) As you inhale, stretch your arms straight behind your head and legs straight to a diagonal position in front. As you exhale, circle arms around and back to legs while drawing knees back in to the 90-degree angle. Repeat for desired number of reps.\n\n\n\nTargets: transverse abdominus, rectus abdominus\n\n\n\nPlank To Balance Plank\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nHow to perform plank to balance plank:\n\n\n\n1) Begin in a plank position with your shoulders over your wrists and your body in a straight line.\n\n\n\n2) Simultaneously raise your right hand off and left foot off the floor, squeezing abs and glutes.\n\n\n\nTargets: transverse abdominus\n\n\n\nAb Exercise FAQs\n\n\n\nWhich ab exercise is the most effective?\n\n\n\nThe bicycle crunch is the most effective abdominal workout, according to a study by the American Council on Exercise.
