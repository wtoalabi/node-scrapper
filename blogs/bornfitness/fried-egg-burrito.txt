"Burritos are fun, versatile, and easy to make. A single burrito is enough for a whole meal and works for breakfast, lunch, or dinner.\nWhat You Need\nFried Egg Burrito Ingredients\n\n2 cloves garlic, finely chopped\n1 cup onion, diced\n1 can low sodium black beans, drained (not rinsed)\n1/2 cup pre-cooked rice (leftovers work great for this meal and saves time)\n1 tortilla or burrito wrap (suggested: look for 4g or more of fiber per serving)\n2 eggs, fried (or cooked however you like them)\nPlum tomatoes, diced\nFresh basil, chopped\nAvocado (optional)\n\nSauce Ingredients\n1/4 cup non-fat plain Greek Yogurt\nHot sauce\n2 Tbsp water\nHow To\n\nIn a skillet on medium heat, soften garlic and onions for 3 minutes. I use a splash of water in place of oil or butter.\nAdd black beans. Cook for another 3-5 minutes.\nWhile veggies are cooking, prepare your eggs the way you like them.\nFor the sauce combine yogurt + hot sauce + water.\nAssemble the burrito with a spoonful of rice, a spoonful of black bean mixture, a handful of tomatoes, eggs, basil, sauce.\n\n\nNatalie SabinNatalie is a M.S. in Nutrition & Performance candidate; anticipated completion December 2021.
