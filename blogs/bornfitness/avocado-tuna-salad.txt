"Most delicious foods are not inherently unhealthy–they are just made that way. So the goal when creating an enjoyable diet isn’t to toss out your favorites; it’s to reinvent them in a way that your stomach and tastebuds can agree.\nThis recipe for avocado tuna salad, created by Liv Langdon, Born Fitness meal prep specialist and founder of Liv Lang & Prosper, is as easy on the wallet as much as it is to make. In less than 10 minutes, you can transform the boring classic tuna salad while packing in heart-healthy monounsaturated fats from the avocado and muscle-building protein.\nEnjoy the tuna salad in an tortilla and toast in the oven for 5 minutes, or add it to a bed of spinach with sliced tomatoes for a lower carb meal. For added flavor, simply top with balsamic vinaigrette.\nThe goal when creating an enjoyable diet isn’t to toss out your favorites; it’s to reinvent them in a way that your stomach and tastebuds can agree.\nThe meal also happens to be perfect for work because the acidity in the lemon will also cut down the fishy smell and taste of the tuna, so you won’t almost kill your co-worker’s nostrils when you open your meal.\nAvocado Tuna Salad Ingredients\n\n1 4-5-oz. can albacore tuna\n½ ripe avocado, mashed\n½ lemon, juiced\n½ tsp mustard powder\n¼ tsp granulated garlic powder\nDash of Himalayan sea salt\nDash of black pepper\n2 tbsp almond slivers, toasted\n\nDirections\n\nRinse the tuna—after opening the can, of course—under cold running water for about a minute. This helps to clean out some of the preservatives used in canning and also will help decrease that fishy smell and taste.\nIn a large bowl, combine all the ingredients except for the almond slivers.\nUsing a large fork, break up the chunks of tuna, and combine all ingredients.\nTop with the toasted almond slivers for an added texture and nice crunchy bite.\nMakes one serving.\n\n\nNutritional Information & Macros\nDietary Information: Gluten free, Paleo, Low carb, Pescetarian, Dairy/Lactose free, No trans fat\nMacros per serving:\n\n357 calories\n19g fat\n10g carbs\n36.5g protein\n\nTotal breakdown of ingredients:\nTuna (4 oz.): 137 calories, 1g fat, 0g carbs, 32g protein\nAvocado: 118 calories, 10g fat, 6g carbs, 1g protein\nAlmonds: 102 calories, 8g fat, 4g carbs, 3.5g protein\nTotal: 357 calories, 19g fat, 10g carbs, 36.5g protein\nAdam BornsteinAdam Bornstein is a New York Times bestselling author and, according to The Huffington Post, “one of the most inspiring sources in all of health and fitness.” An award-winning writer and editor, Bornstein was the Fitness and Nutrition editor for Men’s Health, Editorial Director at LIVESTRONG.com, and a columnist for SHAPE, Men’s Fitness, and Muscle & Fitness.
