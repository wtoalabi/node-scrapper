"When it comes to “go-to” meals, stir fry’s tend to be a staple option. They’re quick, loaded with protein and vegetables, easy to make, and always taste incredible. This recipe offers a little extra flavor in the form of a unique mix of ginger and sriracha. The combination will leave you pleasantly surprises. (Or if you don’t like either ingredient, simply remove them and the dish still tastes great.)\nBorn Fitness Recipes: Beef and Vegetable Stir Fry\nFitness goal: Muscle gain or fat loss\nAllergies: soy (from soy sauce), sriracha (for people allergic to hot stuff…kidding, of course)\nPaleo friendly: Yes\nHow to make it vegetarian friendly: Substitute beef for tofu, seitan, tempeh, or lentils\nPrep time: 10 minutes\nCook time: 15 minutes\nStir Fry Ingredients\n\n1/2 pound flank steak, sliced 1/4” thick\n3 teaspoons soy sauce\n1 tablespoon grated fresh ginger\n2 cloves garlic, minced\n2 stalks of bok choy\n2 handfuls spinach\n2 medium carrots, thinly sliced\n1 stalk broccoli\n1 medium onion, chopped\n1 medium red bell pepper, thinly sliced\n8 ounces snow peas\n1-2 tablespoons sriracha\nRice (optional)\n\nHow to make it\nToss the steak with 2 teaspoons of the soy sauce. Add oil in a large nonstick or cast-iron skillet over medium-high heat.\nAdd the ginger and garlic. Cook, stirring, for 30 seconds. Add the steak and cook, stirring occasionally, for 3-5 minutes. Transfer to a plate and set aside.\nReturn the skillet to the heat, and add the veggies, except for the snow peas. Cook, stirring occasionally, for 3 minutes, or until the vegetables start to soften. Stir in the snow peas. Cook, stirring occasionally, for 2 minutes.\nAdd the reserved steak and sriracha, and the remaining 1 teaspoon soy sauce. Cook, stirring, for 1-3 minutes, until meat takes on desired level of preparedness. Want some extra carbs?
