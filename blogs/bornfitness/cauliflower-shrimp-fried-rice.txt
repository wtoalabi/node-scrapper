"Fact: I am inherently dubious about cauliflower rice. (It is not rice, people)\nFact: This dish makes me a believer!\nIngredients\nYields: 4 servings\n\n1 tablespoon sesame oil (the flavor is rich and more fragrant than olive oil)\n1/2 cup yellow onion\n1 clove garlic, minced\n1 large crown of cauliflower, run through a food processor to make a fine grain “rice”\n2 cups carrots, diced\n2 cups fish or frozen peas\n2 eggs, scrambled\n2 dozen fresh or frozen shrimp, tails off\n1 tablespoon low sodium soy sauce or liquid amino\n\nHow to:\n\nIn a large skillet, cook garlic and onion in sesame oil on medium heat for 3 minutes.\nAdd cauliflower, carrots, peas, and uncooked scrambled eggs to the skillet. Combine & cook for 2 more minutes.\nAdd shrimp and liquid aminos (or soy sauce). Cook until shrimp are pink and vegetables are tender.\nServe it up in your favorite bowl.\n\n\nNatalie SabinNatalie is a M.S. in Nutrition & Performance candidate; anticipated completion December 2021.
