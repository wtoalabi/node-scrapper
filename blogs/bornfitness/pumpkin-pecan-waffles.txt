"This pumpkin pecan waffle recipe will satisfy your sweet tooth. Enjoy for breakfast, brunch, or a mid-day snack. Pro tip: make them ahead of time, throw them in the freezer, and pop ’em in the toaster for a quick grab-and-go snack.\nYIELDS 4 SERVINGS\nIngredients\n\n1 cup pumpkin puree (NOT pumpkin pie filling)\n3 large eggs\n2 Tbsp butter, melted\n1 teaspoon vanilla extract\n2 3/4 cups milk or milk substitute\n2 1/2 cups 100% whole wheat or whole grain flour*\n2 teaspoons ground cinnamon\n1 teaspoon nutmeg\n2 tsp baking powder\n1/2 teaspoon salt\nTopping: chopped pecans\nPure maple syrup and whipped cream(optional)\n\n*Wheat flour has a slightly darker color than traditional white flour. It includes the whole grain which makes it a little more nutrient-dense and fiber-rich. Double-check the label when shopping—technically all flour comes from wheat so you want to look for 100% whole wheat or whole grain flour.\nHow To Make Pumpkin Pecan Waffles\n\nIn a large bowl combine all wet ingredients: puree, eggs, melted butter, extract, milk.\nWhisk until well combined.\nAdd all dry ingredients: spices, baking powder, and flour.\nCook waffles on a preheated waffle iron according to manufacture instructions.\nServe with favorite toppings.\n\nNutritional Information & Macros\nMacros per serving (before maple syrup, whipped cream, and chopped pecans):\n\nCalories: 449\nProtein: 15.7 g\nFat: 12.7 g\nCarbs: 62.3 g\n\n\nAdam BornsteinAdam Bornstein is a New York Times bestselling author and, according to The Huffington Post, “one of the most inspiring sources in all of health and fitness.” An award-winning writer and editor, Bornstein was the Fitness and Nutrition editor for Men’s Health, Editorial Director at LIVESTRONG.com, and a columnist for SHAPE, Men’s Fitness, and Muscle & Fitness.
