"Jicama (Hi-ku-mah) – root vegetable that is juicy and crunchy, with a slightly sweet and nutty flavor. Tastes like a cross between a potato and a pear… And it’s an excellent source of fiber and vitamin C!\nWhy I love it: Jicama is super versatile and found at most grocery stores. Can be eaten raw as a snack, in salads, or cooked in stir fry. This jicama slaw has the fresh taste of summer with cilantro and a crisp mix of veggies.\nIngredients\nYields: 6-8 servings\n\n2 medium jicama (about the size of a potato), peeled and julienned\n1 cup red cabbage, julienned\n2-3 small radishes, peel ON and julienned\n1 large carrot, ribbon cut with a vegetable peeler\n1/3 cup cilantro (more if you love it), chopped\n2 Tablespoons each avocado oil, rice vinegar, red wine vinegar\nPinch of salt\n\nDirections:\n\nCombine all vegetables in a bowl.\nMix in oil, vinegars, and salt.\nSet in fridge for 30 minutes to combine flavors.\nServe as a side dish or top on your favorite grilled protein.\n\n \n\nNatalie SabinNatalie is a M.S. in Nutrition & Performance candidate; anticipated completion December 2021.
