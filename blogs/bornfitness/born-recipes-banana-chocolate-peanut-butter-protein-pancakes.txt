"I don’t eat breakfast very often, but breakfast foods are one of my favorite things to eat. From eggs and chicken sausage, to waffles, pancakes, and french toast, it’s hard to go wrong with any morning meal favorite. The problem? Many carb loaded breakfast foods clock in at thousands of calories, and there’s nothing about them that would even come close to qualifying at healthy.\nEnter protein pancakes, and in particular chocolate peanut butter protein pancakes.\nThis recipe has everything you want: The carb-loaded goodness of pancakes, but with a nutrient profile that makes you go, “I can actually eat these guilt-free!”\nEnjoy these on a weekend, before or after a workout, or any time you just want to enjoy breakfast without a second thought.\nChocolate Peanut Butter Protein Pancakes\nIngredients\n\n1 scoop whey protein powder (approximately 25 grams protein) [for mine, I used 2 scoops of Biotrust vanilla]\n2 tablespoons almond flour\n2 tablespoons non-fat greek yogurt\n1 teaspoon baking soda\n1 tablespoon chocolate peanut butter (you can sub out for regular peanut butter)\n4 egg whites\n1 ripe banana (medium sized)\nOptional topping: additional sliced banana\n\nDirections\nStep 1: Combine all ingredients into a mixer and blend together until thick and creamy consistency.\nStep 2: Set a burner on medium high heat and coat the pan with butter or low calorie spray.\nStep 3: Pour pancake batter into palm size onto skillet or pan.\n\nStep 4: Flip pancakes and then lower the heat to medium-low.\n\nStep 5: Plate your pancakes and serve.\nNutrition Information:\n[Macros for the entire serving size]\n430 calories\n45 grams protein\n30 grams carbs (without added sliced banana)\n13 grams fat\nAdam BornsteinAdam Bornstein is a New York Times bestselling author and, according to The Huffington Post, “one of the most inspiring sources in all of health and fitness.” An award-winning writer and editor, Bornstein was the Fitness and Nutrition editor for Men’s Health, Editorial Director at LIVESTRONG.com, and a columnist for SHAPE, Men’s Fitness, and Muscle & Fitness.
