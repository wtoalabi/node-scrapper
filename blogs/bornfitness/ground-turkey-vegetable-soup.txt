"Umami is one of the 5 recognized flavors of food (sweet, sour, bitter, salty, and umami). It is a savory taste that can be described as pleasant, brothy, meaty, and mouthwatering.\nThe best part? Umami flavor could help you feel more satiated (feel fuller) and feel more satisfied after a meal. Try this easy ground turkey and vegetable soup seasoned with liquid aminos for that umami punch.\nSoup Ingredients\n\n1 lb ground lean ground turkey, cooked\n1 cup white onion, chopped\n2 cloves garlic, minced\n4 cups low sodium chicken or vegetable broth\n2 cups water\n1/3 cup uncooked basmati rice\n3 tablespoons liquid aminos (or low sodium soy sauce)\n1 tablespoon sesame oil\n2 medium carrots, peeled and diced\n1 cup corn (cut off the cob tastes the best!)\n1 cup frozen peas\n2 cups greens. Either raw spinach (stems removed) OR raw kale (chopped)\nGreen onions, sliced (optional for topping)\n\nDirections\n\nCook turkey in a pan with onion and garlic.\nCombine the following in a medium/large pot: cooked turkey mix, broth, water, uncooked rice, liquid aminos (or soy sauce), sesame oil, carrots, and corn.\nBring to a simmer then cover with a lid. On medium/low heat simmer until rice is tender (approximately 15 minutes).\nAdd frozen peas and greens and cook for 3-5 more minutes on low heat until greens are wilted and peas are thawed.\nServe with green onion topping.\n\n\nAdam BornsteinAdam Bornstein is a New York Times bestselling author and, according to The Huffington Post, “one of the most inspiring sources in all of health and fitness.” An award-winning writer and editor, Bornstein was the Fitness and Nutrition editor for Men’s Health, Editorial Director at LIVESTRONG.com, and a columnist for SHAPE, Men’s Fitness, and Muscle & Fitness.
